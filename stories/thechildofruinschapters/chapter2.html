The Child of Ruin

Chapter 2: The Child Among the Dead

The journey through the mountains lasted well into the night.

Snow had begun to fall.

Small white flakes drifted from the dark sky, settling upon the old man’s cloak as he walked along narrow mountain paths.

The child remained asleep in his arms.

The old man glanced down occasionally.

The boy’s breathing was weak but steady.

A miracle.

Or perhaps a mystery.

The old man wasn’t sure which.


The village had been destroyed.

Every person he had found there had been dead.

Yet somehow this child had survived.

Not merely survived.

Endured.

Alone.

The old man tightened his cloak around the boy, shielding him from the cold wind.

Whatever questions he had could wait until morning.

For now, the child needed warmth.

At last, a small cabin appeared through the snowfall.

Built against the mountainside, it stood alone among the trees.

Simple.

Weathered.

Home.

The old man pushed open the wooden door.

Warm air greeted him immediately.

A fire crackled quietly within the stone fireplace.


The cabin was modest.

A table.

Several chairs.

A bed.

Shelves filled with tools, books, and old supplies.

Nothing luxurious.

Everything practical.


The old man gently placed the child upon the bed.

For a moment, he simply stared.

The boy looked even smaller beneath the cabin’s warm light.

His skin was covered in soot and dirt.

Ash clung to his hair.

The old man frowned.

Something felt strange.

The child had been found buried beneath rubble.

Yet there were no visible wounds.

No burns.

No broken bones.

No scars.

Nothing.

As if the destruction had somehow passed around him.

The old man shook his head.

There would be time to think later.

Right now, the boy needed food and rest.
