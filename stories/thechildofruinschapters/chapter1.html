The Child of Ruin

Chapter 1: Ashes and Silence

The wind carried the scent of smoke.

Not the comforting smell of firewood burning on a cold night.

Not the smell of a family’s hearth.

This was different.

Heavy.

Bitter.

The smell of death.


A lone figure walked along a dirt road stretching through the wilderness.

An old man.

His clothes were simple and worn from years of travel.

A weathered cloak hung loosely over his shoulders while a wooden staff rested in his right hand.

His hair had long since turned silver, and wrinkles lined his face.

Yet despite his frail appearance, his steps remained steady.

Unshaken.


His eyes scanned the horizon.

There should have been a village here.

He remembered passing through it years ago.

Small.

Poor.

Forgettable.

But peaceful.

Farmers worked the fields.

Children ran through the streets.

The villagers had welcomed him warmly despite possessing little themselves.


Now silence greeted him instead.

The old man’s brows furrowed.

The closer he walked, the stronger the smell became.

Smoke.

Blood.

Rot.

None of it belonged.

His grip tightened around the staff.

Something was wrong.

Very wrong.


Soon the village came into view.

Or rather, what remained of it.

The old man stopped.

For a long moment, he simply stared.

The wooden palisade surrounding the settlement had collapsed.

Several homes were little more than blackened skeletons.

Others had been crushed entirely.

The fields beyond the village were scorched.

No animals.

No voices.

No movement.

Only ruin.


A cold wind swept through the empty streets.

Ash danced through the air like gray snow.

The old man slowly entered the village.

Every step revealed another scene of destruction.

Broken carts.

Shattered tools.

Burned homes.

The remains of a life abruptly stolen.

His expression darkened.


This wasn’t the work of bandits.

Bandits stole.

Bandits looted.

Bandits left survivors.

This...

This was slaughter.
