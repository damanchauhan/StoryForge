The Child of Ruin

Prologue: The Prophecy of Light and Shadow

The world stood on the edge of collapse.

War had consumed entire kingdoms.

Once-prosperous lands had become graveyards.

Villages vanished overnight.

Cities burned.

Countless lives were swallowed by the endless conflict spreading across the continent.


At the center of every tale of destruction stood a single figure.

Kaeldras Drakhar.

The Demon King.

His armies advanced without pause.

His banners appeared wherever hope died.


Human kingdoms united.

Knight orders marched.

Saints offered prayers.

Yet the tide of war refused to change.

For the first time in centuries, humanity feared extinction.


And so they prayed.

Not for victory.

Not for glory.

But for salvation.

Within the Holy Kingdom, the Grand Cathedral overflowed with people.

The streets surrounding it were packed so tightly that movement was almost impossible.

Knights stood shoulder to shoulder.

Priests filled every hall.

Commoners gathered in silence.

Every face carried the same expression.

Desperation.


The High Pontiff stood before the cathedral's gates, leading one final prayer.

Thousands lowered their heads.

The city fell silent.

Then the heavens answered.

The clouds parted.

A pillar of golden light descended from the sky.

Gasps spread throughout the crowd.

Many fell to their knees.

Others stared in awe.

Golden feathers drifted through the air.


And from within the light emerged a figure.

Six radiant wings stretched behind him.

White robes shimmered beneath the sunlight.

His presence alone seemed enough to calm the fears of those around him.

An angel.

A messenger from the heavens.
