The Child of Ruin

Chapter 4: Learning to Live

The mountains were quiet that morning.

Sunlight filtered through the cabin windows while snow slowly melted from the trees outside.

For once, everything was peaceful.

Aldric should have known it wouldn’t last.


“Rowan.”

The child looked up.

Aldric pointed at himself.

“Aldric.”

Rowan blinked.

“Aldric.”

The child continued staring.

“Aldric.”

A long pause followed.

Then Rowan opened his mouth.

“Aaa.”

Aldric sighed.

“No.”

Rowan smiled.

“Aaa.”

“Still no.”

The smile widened.

Aldric rubbed his forehead.

“This may take longer than expected.”

Rowan giggled.

The old man immediately forgot what he was annoyed about.

Breakfast wasn’t much better.

Aldric placed a bowl of soup on the table.

Rowan’s eyes lit up immediately.

The child pointed.

“Num num!”

Aldric froze.

Slowly.

Very slowly.

He looked at Rowan.

Rowan pointed harder.

“Num num!”

The old man stared.

“You can say that?”

“Num num!”

“I’ve spent days teaching you my name.”

“Num num!”

“Not even close.”

Rowan laughed.

Aldric leaned back in his chair.

Defeated.

Completely defeated.

By a toddler.

The following days settled into a routine.

Aldric gathered wood.

Cooked meals.

Cleaned the cabin.

Rowan followed him everywhere.

At first, the old man thought it was coincidence.

Then he tested it.

He walked to the fireplace.

Rowan followed.

He walked to the table.

Rowan followed.

He stepped outside.

A few moments later, Rowan appeared at the doorway.

Watching him.

Waiting.


The old man sighed.

“You know.”

Rowan tilted his head.

“You’re worse than a puppy.”

The child smiled.

Aldric pointed at him.

“Exactly that look.”

The smile grew wider.

The old man lost the argument immediately.

One afternoon, Aldric decided to gather firewood.

Winter was slowly ending, but mountain nights remained cold.

He grabbed an axe and moved toward the door.

Rowan immediately followed.

“No.”

The old man pointed toward the floor.

“Stay.”

Rowan blinked.

Aldric pointed again.

“Stay.”

The child sat down.

Aldric narrowed his eyes.

Suspicious.

Rowan continued sitting.

Looking completely innocent.

The old man wasn’t convinced.

Still, after a few moments, he stepped outside.

Surely nothing could happen during a simple trip for firewood.

Aldric had barely reached the trees when he heard it.

A cry.

Loud.

Panicked.

Heartbroken.

The sound cut through the mountain air.

The old man’s heart nearly stopped.

The axe fell from his hand.

For a brief moment, every horrible possibility flashed through his mind.

Then he was running.

Not walking.

Running.

Branches snapped beneath his feet.

Snow scattered behind him.

The cry came again.

Louder.

Desperate.

Aldric reached the cabin.

The door burst open.

His eyes immediately searched the room.

Looking for danger.

Looking for injury.

Looking for blood.

Instead, he found a bucket.

An ordinary wooden bucket.

Sitting upside down in the middle of the floor.

It wobbled.

The crying came from inside.

Aldric froze.

Silence.

The bucket wobbled again.

“Waaahhh!”
